Mind & Philosophy 🧠

Books 📖

Steering the Craft

by Ursula K. Le Guin

Status: Planned 🕒 | Original Year: 1998 | Genre: Writing, Craft

Not Yet Rated View on Goodreads

A technical workbook that zeroes in on syntax, rhythm, and narrative voice—without fluff. It's designed to help writers refine their prose instincts.

**Why I want to read it:** I want to refine my prose instincts and tighten the relationship between form and meaning in my writing. Le Guin's insights are invaluable.

The War of Art

by Steven Pressfield

Status: Planned 🕒 | Original Year: 2002 | Genre: Creativity, Self-Help

Not Yet Rated View on Goodreads

A manifesto on creative Resistance, arguing that the biggest enemy to our creative aspirations is internal self-sabotage.

**Why I want to read it:** I’m reading it to unpack my internal barriers and build creative discipline without losing soul. Less about productivity, more about grit and psychological warfare with the self.

Metaphors We Live By

by George Lakoff & Mark Johnson

Status: Planned 🕒 | Original Year: 1980 | Genre: Linguistics, Philosophy

Not Yet Rated View on Goodreads

A foundational text arguing that metaphors aren’t just literary devices—they fundamentally shape our logic, politics, and perception of reality.

**Why I want to read it:** I’m especially drawn to how this reframes the way I process meaning in everyday language, belief, and narrative. It promises to deepen my understanding of communication.

Atomic Habits

by James Clear

Status: Planned 🕒 | Original Year: 2018 | Genre: Self-Help, Productivity

Not Yet Rated View on Goodreads

An easy-to-understand, practical guide on how to build good habits and break bad ones, focusing on small changes that yield remarkable results.

**Why I want to read it:** I'm looking for actionable strategies to improve my daily routines and create systems that support long-term goals. This book comes highly recommended for its clarity and effectiveness.

Mindset: The New Psychology of Success

by Carol S. Dweck

Status: Planned 🕒 | Original Year: 2006 | Genre: Psychology, Self-Help

Not Yet Rated View on Goodreads

Explores the power of mindset—how our beliefs about our abilities and potential profoundly impact our success and happiness. It distinguishes between a fixed mindset and a growth mindset.

**Why I want to read it:** Understanding the concepts of fixed versus growth mindsets is crucial for personal and professional development. I'm keen to learn how to cultivate a more resilient and adaptable approach to challenges.

Articles 📝

"The Busy Trap"

by Tim Kreider

Status: Completed ✅ | Original Year: 2012 | Genre: Essay, Social Commentary

⭐⭐⭐⭐ Read Article

A sharp critique of performative busyness and our obsession with self-worth through work. It hits close to home.

**Why I recommend it:** This essay was a major wake-up call for me. It perfectly articulates the difference between genuinely being productive and merely *feeling* busy. Sometimes I need reminders that presence matters more than productivity.

"Deep Work" (Excerpt)

by Cal Newport

Status: On Deck 🎧 | Original Year: 2016 (Book) | Genre: Productivity, Self-Help

Not Yet Rated Read Excerpt

An excerpt focusing on the value of focused, distraction-free time for producing high-quality work in a world designed to derail your attention.

**Why I'm exploring it:** I’m exploring it as a counterweight to my fractured attention and to rethink how I approach deep personal work. It's a foundational text for serious productivity.

Stories & Creativity 📚

Light Novels 📖

Ascendance of a Bookworm

by Miya Kazuki

Status: Completed ✅ | Original Year: 2013 | Genre: Isekai, Fantasy, Slice of Life

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Read on J-Novel Club

A quiet, obsessive girl is reborn into a medieval world with one goal: make books. What starts as a personal passion spirals into systemic change—through libraries, education, and logistics.

**Why I recommend it:** I keep returning to it for how it validates intellectual hunger as a world-shaping force. It's a surprisingly deep dive into economics and societal change through the lens of a simple passion.

Tensei Shitara Slime Datta Ken

by Fuse

Status: Completed ✅ | Original Year: 2013 | Genre: Isekai, Fantasy, Kingdom Building

⭐⭐⭐⭐ Read on J-Novel Club

What if utopia started with a slime? It’s a surprisingly detailed take on multicultural governance, alliances, and interspecies diplomacy—disguised as action fantasy.

**Why I recommend it:** I read it for joy, but stayed for the systems. It's a fascinating exploration of community building, resource management, and diplomacy, all wrapped in an engaging fantasy setting.

Manga 漫畫

Houseki no Kuni (Land of the Lustrous)

by Haruko Ichikawa

Status: Completed ✅ | Original Year: 2012 | Genre: Sci-Fi, Fantasy, Existential

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ View on Kodansha

A slow, existential sci-fi where gem-bodied beings drift through time, identity, and ruin, constantly battling a mysterious enemy from the moon.

**Why I recommend it:** Gorgeous to look at—philosophically haunting beneath. I read it like a visual essay on impermanence, adaptation, and the struggle for identity in a world of constant change.

TV Series 📺

Star Trek (Franchise)

Created by Gene Roddenberry & various

Status: Completed ✅ | Original Year: 1966-Present | Genre: Sci-Fi, Exploration, Philosophy

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Explore Franchise

A vast science fiction universe encompassing multiple television series (TOS, TNG, DS9, Voyager, Discovery, Lower Decks, Strange New Worlds, etc.), films, and books, centered on themes of exploration, diplomacy, and humanity's future.

**Why I recommend it:** Star Trek is more than just sci-fi; it's a hopeful vision of humanity's future, tackling complex ethical and philosophical questions through compelling characters and innovative storytelling. Each series offers a unique perspective on diplomacy, societal challenges, and the pursuit of knowledge. It's a foundational text for utopian thought and exploration.

It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia

Created by Rob McElhenney, Glenn Howerton, Charlie Day

Status: Completed ✅ | Original Year: 2005-Present | Genre: Sitcom, Dark Comedy, Absurdist

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Watch on FX/Hulu

Follows "The Gang"—five dysfunctional, narcissistic, and morally bankrupt friends who run Paddy's Pub in South Philadelphia, consistently scheming their way into outlandish and often disastrous situations.

**Why I recommend it:** It's a masterclass in dark comedy, pushing boundaries with its irreverent humor and commitment to character flaws. The writing is incredibly sharp, and the consistent refusal of the characters to learn or grow is hilariously captivating. It's a brilliant deconstruction of traditional sitcom tropes.
