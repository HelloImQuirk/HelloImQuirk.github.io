Signal in the Dark

Published on July 18, 2025 | Category: Reflection

Self-exploration Personal Growth Well-being Emotional Intelligence Mindfulness Clarity

Why "Signal in the Dark"?

Starting something new is daunting. More than daunting, it's a risk that we all too often aren't willing to take. Humans are socially and biologically designed to move towards comfort (that's why we have all the amazing technology and inventions that we do‚Äîto make life easier). That move towards comfort isn't inherently bad, but it can blind us from the possibilities. And the messier, more undefined a new endeavor seems? Forget it! You might as well not even bother at that point. But that feeling, that rush of excitement and endorphins, that visceral sense of achievement you get when you DO take a leap and it DOES pay off? It's addicting enough to make all the missed opportunities (and the ones that didn't work out) worth it. But how? How do you find your way forward when there is no well-tread path, or you have no experience in what you're trying to do? In my opinion, it's that journey of discovery that is what makes a new endeavor so exciting. Besides, sometimes all you need is a bit of grit and determination. After all, if there's a will there's a way, right?

"This blog is my personal 'something new'. It's my Signal in the Dark."

As we've seen the internet become an increasingly sterile, corporate, bot-infested place, it feels like there are fewer and fewer spaces for genuine human expression and thought. So to me, that signal represents just that. It's about sending a small light, regardless of whether or not someone sees it. Like a lighthouse on a dark and rocky shore, guiding ships to port, letting the sailors know that there is someone out there. In essence, that is what all writing is. It is the author reaching out, hoping that someone connects with the piece, hoping for some kind of connection. And writing without a predetermined outcome or final goal? That's the equivalent of sailing without the lighthouse to guide you.

So why do I feel compelled to write? What fundamental human need does it fulfill for me? I think writing for me is how I build a relationship with myself. After years of self-esteem and mental health issues, my sense of self is... fragmented to say the least. I don't have a clear idea of who I am or what I bring to the table. Writing is how I am beginning to build that idea. If you can't be your own friend, or at least know what kind of person you are, it becomes that much harder to be a good friend to someone else or truly know other people. Writing is a way to heal, a way to say "yes, I DO have my own thoughts and YES they do have value". But above all else, I don't write to be heard (although that is nice), **I write because I** exist‚Äîand all humans that exist want to leave a mark of some kind on someone.

It is my hope that this blog does that. That it can leave a mark, however faint, on another person. That it can leave an indelible mark on someone. I guess what makes this blog different is me and what I want for the space. What this blog really is, is an invitation. An invitation to go on a journey with me: the journey and great experiment of **self-discovery.** Like I said, it is that journey of discovery that makes life so exciting. The process is as important as any actual outcome you might reach.

My Promise: No Ads, No Fa√ßade

Genuine authenticity in a public space such as this means true **unembellishment**. It means presenting yourself without a facade‚Äîto truly just "be yourself" as you are. As clich√© as that might sound, when you try to polish or otherwise obscure your current state, you lose something. That isn't to say that's a bad thing, but you can't truly convey the chaos of self-discovery unless you embody that chaos. It is crucial to the mission of this blog, then, that that chaos is accurately and honestly portrayed with a sense of grace and care. Otherwise, this could become just another blog without originality, and if that innate sense of humanity is lost, what's the point?

"My embrace of the messy is a rejection of that pressure. It's essential to my creative process. I can not think any other way. I can not force myself to be something I am not. My very purpose here is to show that you, reader, are not alone."

Especially in circles of personal relationships, career, family, and **social media**, there can be a pressure (usually from ourselves) to present a finished, complete product. We are taught to market ourselves like some kind of product. My embrace of the messy is a rejection of that pressure. It's essential to my creative process. I can not think any other way. I can not force myself to be something I am not. My very purpose here is to show that you, reader, **are not alone.** Lying to you would defeat that purpose; trying to present myself as a perfectly marketable product would ruin the trust and intention of this space.

It is my hope to cultivate a sort of safe haven, for both myself and anyone who reads this. A space where freedom of thought is respected, opinions are listened to, and everyone's lived experience is genuinely considered. I can make you a few promises then about my writing's origin and intention:

  • Sanctuary over sales pitch:

    My purpose here is self-exploration, not commercial gain. My words are offered freely. You won't find ads, affiliate links, or overt monetization here.

  • Unfiltered and Unfinished Thoughts Welcome:

    This is a space for real, truthful, genuine initial thought no matter how raw or messy. It's for the exploration of the most fundamental human questions, self-discovery of ideas, and sharing of experience.

  • Respect for Every Perspective:

    While this blog is for the expression of my own lived experience, perspective, and thoughts, the foundation of it all is that every lived experience holds value. Diverse opinions are to be genuinely considered, and respectful dialogue is the key to that.

  • An Experiment in Being Human:

    This is a live experiment in understanding myself and the world in which I live. I promise to be honest and upfront about myself, my struggles, discoveries, and process. It's not about having all the answers, but in having the courage to ask the questions... and share the journey of finding them without pretense.

What I'm Risking by Writing Publicly (And Why It's Worth It)

Here's a piece of honesty: this is kind of nerve-wracking. What if my thoughts and ideas are rejected? What if they're stolen? I'm exposing a lot of myself by embarking on this project. It can be a big leap to take.

Modes of Writing

Understanding different approaches to personal writing.

Expressive Writing

The raw, emotional writing that focuses on your personal feelings and experiences. Think of it like journaling. It's a great way to release emotional tension, promote catharsis, and improve mood and health.

Reflective Writing

A more analytical form of writing that explores personal thoughts, actions, and encourages you to learn from experiences. It's a way to integrate your lived experience into your persona and identity. It builds self-awareness, critical thinking, and personal growth.

Life Writing

The most narrative of the three. It's writing about real-life events (e.g., memoirs, personal essays, autobiographies, etc.). It preserves your memory, shapes your identity through writing about your life, and connects you with others by telling your story.

This Is for You If...

This blog serves as a journal of sorts‚Äîan outlet for writing that anyone can read. When it comes to writing, there is often so much pressure‚Äîto be a certain way, to farm for engagement, to sell an idea or product. I am doing none of that. The very public nature of this "journal" allows for me to send my thoughts into the ether and to experiment with my own voice and ideas, as unfinished and raw as they might be.

This blog, then, is for you if you've ever felt lost, at war with yourself, or lost in the chaos of life. I hope that my writing can offer you, at the very least, some solace in your own personal struggles. Maybe even some insight. It's important to note that I am not here to prescribe or dictate: what works for me may not work for you. My discoveries and insights might be too self-specific sometimes. But I think there is a real sense of peace that can come from knowing that someone else is going through it too.

I think that the search for meaning, the pursuit of the self, and the desire to know, grow, and be the best version of yourself possible are some of the most fundamental parts of the human experience. Maybe you've felt this, too. Maybe you've also felt like you need to work on yourself, or have questioned who or what kind of person you are. I think this is where we can truly connect, dear reader. Even if the path we each walk is ours alone, there is great solace in knowing that you are not the only one to have walked it before. Someone has gone before you to clear the way, as you clear the way for those behind you.

"No one can determine your value but yourself."

If nothing else, I hope that when you encounter my "signal in the dark" that you walk away with a sense of reassurance. You have to give yourself the permission to be messy. But the very least I can do is validate that your struggle is real and worth it. Hopefully, by witnessing my journey, you can be inspired to start your own. To begin asking these questions of yourself, whether through writing (privately or publicly) or through whatever channels your emotions best.

I think that through the witnessing or even personal experience of self-exploration can be a truly profound experience. Humans are incredibly surprising creatures, and you never know just what you might find or what your true potential is because that is something you have an active role in deciding. Putting your thoughts into words, no matter how long, short, or messy, is an incredibly healing experience. At the very least, cathartic and revealing. By doing so, you process and review what happens in real time, rather than overthinking it in your head for hours you can get it all out. And witnessing someone else do it can be inspiring, or at least give you the courage to try it yourself. And that is my ultimate hope: that whoever reads this might begin asking themselves the hard questions, might start writing, and maybe even discover something new about themselves.
